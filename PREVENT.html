PREVENT

What is the PREVENT Duty? ‘The Prevent duty, is the duty in the Counter-Terrorism and Security Act 2015 on specified authorities, in the exercise of their functions, to have due regard to the need to prevent people from being drawn into terrorism.’ (Department for Education, 2015) The Home Office defines radicalisation as ‘the process by which a person comes to support terrorism and ​extremist ideologies associated with terrorist groups’​ (Revised Prevent Duty Guidance for England and Wales, issued on 12th March 2015 and revised on 16th July 2015, definition). Early Years practitioners all have a responsibility to promote British Values These are; Democracy Rule of Law Individual Liberty Mutual Respect and Tolerance Whilst these might seem to be complex ideas for small children, they are addressed through the everyday practice of the Early Years Foundation Stage (EYFS). Democracy • Making decisions together • Knowing our views count and appreciating the views of others • Reflecting the Child’s Voice • Turning taking and collaboration Individual liberty • Supporting children to develop their awareness of and sense of self • Building confidence • Exploring and respecting individual feelings Rule of law • Understanding rules and the need for them • Knowing that actions have impacts • Developing a sense of right and wrong, within community expectations Mutual tolerance and respect • Engaging with the wider community • Tolerance and appreciation of own and others cultures • Developing tolerant behaviours such as sharing or respecting different opinions • Providing resources that challenge stereotypes across a range of areas There are lots of useful sources of further information out there. Here are a few to get you started: Ideas of how to incorporate British Values into your everyday practise: https://www.tts-group.co.uk/blog/2016/05/19/incorporate-british-values-everyday-play.html PACEY breakdown the requirements of british values and how they apply to early years: https://www.pacey.org.uk/news-and-views/pacey-blog/2015/september-2015/fundamental-british-values-in-the-early-years/ Some practical examples of how everyday practise can be used to promote British Values in the early years: https://www.brighton-hove.gov.uk/sites/default/files/migrated/article/inline/Promoting%20fundamental%20British%20Values%20for%20Mary.pdf What are British Values and how can you bring them into your childcare setting: https://www.nurseryresources.org/articles/british-values/teaching-british-values-for-eyfs/ EYFS best practise - all about British Values: https://www.nurseryworld.co.uk/features/article/eyfs-best-practice-all-about-british-values The government guidance on what is expected can be found here; https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/protecting-children-from-radicalisation-the-prevent-duty There are a whole range of examples of extremism and it is important as practitioners that we do not stereotype or assume that a particular group is more likely to be vulnerable to radicalisation. Examples of extremism may include groups with extreme political views such as far right groups, animal rights groups, environmental extremism, and religious extremism.
